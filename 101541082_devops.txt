DevOps is a set of practices and tools that combines software development and IT operations. It's a cultural philosophy that encourages collaboration and automation to improve the speed and quality of software delivery. 
How DevOps works
Collaboration
DevOps teams work together to eliminate roadblocks and improve how they create, deploy, and monitor software. 
Automation
DevOps uses technology to automate processes that were previously manual and slow. 
Integration
DevOps integrates the work of development and operations teams into a single, continuous process. 
Benefits of DevOps
Faster delivery: DevOps helps organizations deliver applications and services faster than traditional software development processes. 
Improved quality: DevOps helps organizations build, test, and release software that is more reliable. 
Better communication: DevOps encourages better communication and collaboration between teams...
DevOps history
The DevOps movement began around 2007 when the software development and IT operations communities started to question the traditional software development model.
